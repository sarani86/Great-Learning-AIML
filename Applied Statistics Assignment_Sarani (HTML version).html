Q3f. Measure of skewness of ‘bmi’, ‘age’ and ‘charges’ columns¶

Skewness is the extent to which the data values are not symmetrical around the mean.

$$skeweness = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n}(x_i - \bar{x})^3} {(n-1)*\sigma^3}$$

Mean > Median : Positive or right skewed distribution

Mean = Median: Zero skewness or symmetrical distribution

Mean < Median : Negative or left skewed distribution

Q3g. Checking the presence of outliers in ‘bmi’, ‘age’ and ‘charges¶

Presence of outliers can be detected by Box-plot diagrams.

Box-plots are diagrams to reprsent the 5 point summary of attributes i.e. the minimum, maximum, 1st Quartile, 2nd Quartile (also called Median) and 3rd Quartile.

The end points of the diagram are called called whiskers. The lower whisker represents the value Q1 - 1.5IQR, while the upper whisker represents the value Q3 + 1.5IQR where Q1 and Q3 are the 1st and 3rd Quartiles respectively and IQR is the Inter Quartile Range is the size of the box and equals Q3-Q1.

Any point beyond these whiskers is termed as an outlier. An outlier could be both towards the lower as well as higher side of the attribute.

    total = len(feature)

        x = p.get_x() + p.get_width() / 2 - 0.05
        y = p.get_y() + p.get_height()

Q4a. Do charges of people who smoke differ significantly from the people who don't?¶

In [15]:

The charges appear different for smokers and non-smokers. We proceed to see if this difference is statistically significant.

Let the random variable X1 denote the charges of smoker with mean 1 and variance 1-square

And the random variable X2 denote the charges of non-smokers with mean 2 and variance 2-square

Null and Alternate Hypothesis¶

The null hypothesis is:

H0 : 1 = 2 (the charges of smokers and non-smokers do not vary significantly)

The alternate hypothesis is:

H1 : 1 2 (the charges of smokers and non-smokers vary significantly)

Defining Parameters¶

Let,

x1 : mean charges of smoker group

x2 : mean charges of non-smoker group

S1 : SD of charges of smokers group

S2 : SD of charges of non-smokers group

n1 : sample size of smoker group

n2 : sample size of non-smoker group

Level of significance, test-statistic, Type of test and Degrees of Freedom¶

Here,

The test is two sample t-test for independent samples

We use level of significance as 5% i.e. = 0.05

This is a two-tailed test

The degrees of freedom is n1+n2-2

Assumptions¶

  • The variances S1-sqaure and S2-square to be equal and the pooled variance is given by
$$Sp^2 = \frac{(n_1-1)*S_1^2+(n_2-1)*S_2^2}{n_1+n_2-2}$$

If we do not consider the assumption then we need to consider the Welch test.

  • The population follow a normal distribution

  • The data points are random and are independent of each other

The test can be done by two methods:

1. Critical Region Method¶

We compute the value of t-statistic where

$$ t-statistic = \frac{\bar{x1}-\bar{x2}}{\sqrt{\frac{S_p^2}{n_1}+\frac{S_p^2}{n_2}}} $$

and compare it with t_critical for = 0.05 for two-tailed test and df = n1+n1-2 from tables.

The rejection region is:

t-statistic < -t_critical or t-statistic > t_critical

Under this condition we reject the null hypothesis and conclude that from given data the charges of smokers and non-smokers appear to be different.

2. p-value Method¶

We compute the p-value of the test. If the p-value is less than (= 0.05) we reject the null hypothesis and conclude that from given data the charges of smokers and non-smokers appear to be different.

# Deriving the required values to conduct the test based on critical region method

x1 = insurance[insurance['smoker'] == 'yes'].charges
x2 = insurance[insurance['smoker'] == 'no'].charges

x1_bar = x1.mean()
x2_bar = x2.mean()


n1 = len(x1.index)
n2 = len(x2.index)
dof=n1+n2-2

s1 = np.std(x1,ddof=1)
s2 = np.std(x2,ddof=1)

Sp_square = ((n1-1)*s1**2 + (n2-1)*s2**2)/(n1+n2-2)
t = (x1_bar-x2_bar)/sqrt((Sp_square/n1)+(Sp_square/n2))

t_critical = 1.96

print('Count, Mean and standard deviation of charges of smokers: %3d, %3.2f and %3.2f' % (n1, x1_bar,s1))
print('Count, Mean and standard deviation of charges of non-smokers: %3d, %3.2f and %3.2f' % (n2, x2_bar,s2))
print()
print('The calculated t-statistic is', round(t,4), 'and the t-critical at 0.05 level of significance for two-tailed test and',dof,'dof is',t_critical,'from tables')

print()

if -t_critical < t < t_critical:
    print("Since the test statistic lies in between the critical value points, we fail to reject the null hypothesis")
else:
    print("Since the test statistic lies in the rejection region, we reject the null hypothesis")

Q4c. Is the proportion of smokers significantly different in different genders¶

We want to see if the proportion of smokers in the two genders is significantly different

Null and Alternate Hypothesis¶

The null hypothesis is:

H0 : p1 = p2 (the proportion of smokers in the two genders does not differ significantly)

The alternate hypothesis is:

H1 : p1 p2 (the proportion of smokers in the two genders differ significantly)

where p1 and p2 are proportion of smokers in males and females respectively

Level of significance, test-statistic, Type of test¶

Here, we use the two-tailed test for test of proportion and choose level of significance as 5% ie = 0.05

The test statistic is:

$$ Z = \frac{p1_{hat} - p2_{hat}}{\sqrt{p_0*(1-p_0)*(\frac{1}{n_1}+\frac{1}{n_2})}} $$

where

$$ p_0 = \frac{x_1+x_2}{n_1+n_2} $$

; x1 and x2 being the male and female smokers respectively and n1 and n2 being the total males and females respectively.

p1_hat and p2_hat are estimates of p1 and p2.

By Critical Region Method we reject null hypothesis if:

  • Z< -Z_critical or Z > Z_critical. Here Z_critical is 1.96

and by p-value Method we reject null hypothesis if:

  • p-value is less than 0.05

Assumptions¶

  • The data points are random and independent of each other

The box-plot does not show much significant difference in BMI of females with 0,1 and 2 children. However, we proceed with the Analysis of Variance (ANOVA) to check statistically if there is any difference.

ANOVA is a hypothesis testing technique that tests the equality of more than two population means by examining the variances of samples that are taken.

Assumptions¶

  • All populations involved follow a normal distribution

  • All populations have the same variance

  • The data points are random and independent of one another

Null and Alternate Hypothesis¶

H0 : _c0 = _c1 = _c2

H1 : At least one differs

Here _c0, _c1 and _c2 are the mean BMI of females with 0,1 and 2 children respectively.

Level of Significance¶

We take here 5% level of significance i.e. = 0.05

Test Statistic¶

ANOVA uses F-tests to statistically test the equality of means.

p-value¶

statsmodels.formula.api.ols creates a model from a formula and dataframe

statsmodels.api.sm.stats.anova_lm gives an Anova table for one or more fitted linear models

The number PR(>F) gives the p-value. If p-value < level of significance we reject the null hypothesis
